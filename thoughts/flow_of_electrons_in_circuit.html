How does water know a tap has been opened? Current flows when it can because it can.

Right, but what is causing the flow? It's a potential. In the case of flowing water, high pressure flows spontaneously to lower pressure. Water in the faucet is at a higher pressure than the atmosphere, so when the faucet is opened, the water "sees" a lower pressure and flows.

I believe voltage is the equivalent of pressure . The more voltage the higher the flow of electrons.
