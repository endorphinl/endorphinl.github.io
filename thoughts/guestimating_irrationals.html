They had big tables with values of standard functions (eg the classic Abramowitz and Stegun), and better knowledge of basic algebra to make small calculations quickly.

Also, an interesting factoid: they used to determine integrals by drawing the function, cutting it out, and thenweighing the paper. Pretty clever.

If the integral of a function is the area under the curve, then if they cut out this area and knew the thickness and density of the paper they were using they could estimate the area.
